### 2.2 Strategic Interpretation

The **April 2026 regulatory environment** is characterized by a critical transition: the shift from **regulatory adoption to regulatory enforcement**. Many frameworks enacted in 2023–2025 are now in active enforcement phases, meaning:

> **The window for "good-faith compliance planning" is closing. Regulators across jurisdictions are moving from guidance to penalties.**

Key enforcement patterns observed:
- **Regulators are increasingly coordinating cross-border.** EU-US data transfer mechanisms, AI governance expectations, and incident reporting timelines are converging in spirit even where legal specifics differ.
- **Third-party and supply chain risk** continues to be the dominant theme across nearly every major framework — from DORA's ICT concentration risk provisions to NIS2's supply chain obligations.
- **Board-level accountability** is no longer aspirational. SEC disclosure rules, DORA governance mandates, and the NIST CSF 2.0 "Govern" function collectively create an expectation that cybersecurity is a fiduciary responsibility.
