5) Recommendations for Action
- Governance and oversight
  - Assign executive ownership for cyber risk with clear risk appetite statements for downtime, data loss, and third‑party reliance.
  - Establish a quarterly GRC dashboard to the board with KRIs: critical vulnerabilities aging, MFA coverage, phishing rates, backup recoverability, vendor risk status, incident MTTD/MTTR, and compliance evidence readiness.
  - Refresh the enterprise risk register and align top risks to funded initiatives and control owners.

- Compliance readiness
  - Update the legal and regulatory obligations register and map to policies/controls/evidence; run a focused gap assessment on incident reporting, vendor oversight, and data handling.
  - Pre‑assemble disclosure kits: materiality criteria, communication templates, stakeholder lists, and regulator contact protocols.
  - Conduct a documentation sprint to ensure policies are current, approved, communicated, and supported by procedures and logs.

- Third‑party risk management
  - Re‑tier vendors by criticality and data sensitivity; prioritize top 20 for enhanced due diligence, contract reviews, and control attestations.
  - Add or enforce contractual clauses for security controls, breach notification timelines, software bill of materials (SBOM), and right to audit.
  - Implement continuous monitoring for key vendors and require incident drill participation and dependency mapping (including fourth parties).

- Security control uplift
  - Identity: Achieve 100% MFA coverage for privileged and remote access; deploy phishing‑resistant methods where feasible; tighten joiner/mover/leaver processes.
  - Vulnerability and patching: Adopt risk‑based SLAs (e.g., internet‑facing exploitable vulnerabilities remediated within 7 days); track exceptions; verify with scanning.
  - Endpoint and email: Ensure EDR and anti‑phishing controls are deployed and monitored; run targeted phishing simulations with just‑in‑time training.
  - Cloud hygiene: Apply baseline guardrails (CIS benchmarks), least privilege, encryption at rest/in transit, and logging; remediate public exposure of sensitive storage.
  - Backups and resilience: Verify immutable, segmented backups and conduct quarterly recovery tests for critical systems; document RTO/RPO attainment.

- Data protection
  - Complete a data inventory for critical systems and high‑risk data; enforce classification and retention schedules.
  - Enable audit logging sufficient for breach assessment and regulatory reporting; centralize logs with retention aligned to obligations.
  - Implement DLP and egress monitoring for sensitive data; validate effectiveness through tabletop tests.

- Incident response and crisis management
  - Run an executive tabletop focused on ransomware and third‑party breach scenarios, including disclosure decisioning and customer communications.
  - Define cross‑functional roles (Legal, Compliance, Security, Comms, Procurement) and escalation paths; maintain a single source of truth for evidence.
  - Establish customer and regulator notification playbooks with timing triggers and content approvals.

- 30/60/90‑day action plan
  - 30 days
    - Refresh risk register and regulatory obligations map; assign owners and deadlines.
    - Tier critical vendors and initiate enhanced due diligence for the top tier.
    - Close highest‑risk MFA coverage gaps; validate backup integrity and perform a recovery test.
    - Update incident disclosure playbook; run a focused tabletop.
  - 60 days
    - Remediate exploitable internet‑facing vulnerabilities; implement cloud baseline guardrails and alerting.
    - Execute contract updates for top vendors (security clauses, notification SLAs, SBOM).
    - Complete data inventory for priority systems; enable centralized logging with appropriate retention.
  - 90 days
    - Deploy phishing‑resistant MFA for admins and high‑risk roles; tighten privileged access workflows.
    - Establish continuous monitoring for key vendors; integrate KRIs into the board dashboard.
    - Conduct a cross‑vendor resilience exercise and document business continuity dependencies and workarounds.

- Metrics and KRIs to monitor
  - Percentage of privileged accounts with phishing‑resistant MFA; overall MFA coverage.
  - Mean time to patch exploitable critical vulnerabilities; number aged beyond SLA.
  - Percentage of critical vendors with current assessments, SBOMs, and contract security clauses.
  - Backup recovery success rate and time to recover for critical systems.
  - Incident detection and response times (MTTD/MTTR); number of regulatory notifications made within prescribed timelines.
  - Data retention compliance (systems aligned to policy) and percentage of sensitive repositories with logging enabled.
